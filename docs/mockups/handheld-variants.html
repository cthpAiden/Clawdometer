Handheld — T4 variants: stats on the LCD, ClawdBoy on the shell

Six takes on the same two changes. 1) the 7-day and Fable numbers move off the plastic and onto the pea-green screen, where the rest of the readout already lives. 2) the control-row slot between the D-pad and the jelly buttons stops printing 7D41 FB18 and becomes the ClawdBoy wordmark.

Note — the shipped T4 already prints Clawd Boy as a tiny tag along the bottom edge of the shell. Putting the wordmark in the control row makes that a duplicate, so H1–H5 drop the bottom tag. H6 keeps it and repurposes it as a spec line instead, the way a real console prints its feature blurb under the logo.

Compact tiles carry no wordmark. At 120px the shell has about 74px of control row; the D-pad and the two buttons eat 44 of it, and "ClawdBoy" at a legible size does not fit in what's left — it pushed the buttons off the plastic. The compact tile keeps the shipped T4 control row and shows the wordmark only at full size.

Superseded — nothing on this page is being built. The pea-green screen was replaced by a true two-tone panel and every layout was redrawn in handheld-mono.html, which also carries all four metrics. Kept for the wordmark study at the bottom, which still stands: the control-row treatment picked there is the one the monochrome set uses.
