Handheld · monochrome — a real 2-bit panel, all four numbers on it

Every variant below shows the full payload: the 5-hour window, the weekly, Fable, and the reset countdown. Nothing is dropped at full size.

Two tones, strictly. Inside the bezel there is one glass color and one ink color and no third value. Where something needs a half state — an empty gauge, a de-emphasised row — it gets a 50% checker dither, which is what an actual 2-bit panel does and is still only the same two colors. Polarity flips by swapping the two variables: M1–M5 are ink-on-glass, M6 and M9 are inverted (backlit).

Clawd is recolored here, on purpose. Everywhere else the mascot is locked to #e17956 and the shared stylesheet enforces it with !important. On this screen the body becomes the ink and the eyes become the glass showing through, because an orange sprite would be the one thing on the panel that the panel could not render. This override is scoped to this skin only — no other skin can reach it.
